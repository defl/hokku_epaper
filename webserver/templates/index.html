Dither algorithms

Floyd–Steinberg + hue-aware — FS error diffusion combined with a palette-selection rule that forbids large hue jumps (so warm skin tones don't pick up blue speckles) and chroma-preserving dynamic-range compression (keeps small saturated features like lips and tongues from washing out). Best overall for photos. Default. B&W inputs are auto-detected and rendered with a neutral preset to avoid pink cast.

Atkinson + hue-aware — Same hue-aware palette rule, but with Atkinson's 6/8 error diffusion: softer noise texture, at the cost of slightly muted small saturated features.

Atkinson (no hue correction) — Plain Atkinson against Lab-nearest palette matching. Softer, but warm tones may drift blue.

Floyd–Steinberg (classic) — Full-strength error diffusion, Lab-nearest palette matching. Highest contrast but can show blue speckles on skin.

Switching triggers re-conversion of every image for the new algorithm — can take several seconds per image. Cached renders for the other algorithms are preserved, so switching back is instant.
